About Me

    Hello! I’m William Zujkowski, a cybersecurity engineer with over 15 years of IT experience. My expertise covers vulnerability management, DevSecOps, and cloud security. I love tackling challenges at the intersection of ethical hacking, advanced threat detection, and High-Performance Computing (HPC).

    I’ve worked in corporate, government, and research environments—securing HPC clusters for groundbreaking molecular dynamics at NIH, and supporting the cloud.gov security platform at GSA. My focus has always been on building cross-functional security teams that maintain compliance and confidentiality under tight deadlines. I strive to keep systems resilient against the ever-changing threat landscape.

    When I’m not working, I enjoy exploring AI/ML solutions for automated threat detection, spinning up homelab setups for hands-on learning, and writing about cybersecurity topics for the broader tech community. If you’d like to chat about DevSecOps best practices, HPC infrastructure, or how machine learning can transform security intelligence, let’s connect—I’m always looking for new ways to innovate and grow.
